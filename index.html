CYBERBULLYING

The use of electronic communication to bully a person, typically by sending messages of an intimidating or threatening nature
Powered by w3.css

WHO

Statistically, there are more male cyberbullies than female cyberbullies. This statistic aided by the fact that there are more males that play videogames than females, due to the history of video games being marketed to men. About 40% of individuals would identify themselves of experiencing some form of cyberbullying. This considerable percentage is due to the anonymity of playing online, in which one is able to have different aliases without their personal information being disclosed to fellow players. This creates a sense of invulnerability to consequences of what they say and do, hence the existence of cyberbullying.

WHAT

Cyberbullying is the act of intentionally harming someone emotionally & mentally through the use of the internet. There are many forms of cyberbullying with the main form being the sending of messages to a specific indivisual. But this act can scale anywhere from making a post about a specific person to sending them a direct message online to cause emotional distress or disease to an indivisual.

WHERE

Cyberbullying is of course most seen online throughout chatrooms, gaming, social networkds and more forms of communication between persons online. These interactions online can have offline effects throughout these victims lives.

WHEN

Cyberbullying takes place online, especially on social media platforms such as Facebook.com, Instagram, and Snapchat. They host millions of users, which can use these platforms to bully. Cyberbullying in videogames has also become a very serious matter, with players using and creating terms that degrade race, gender, sexuality, and more. There has been a rise in cyberbullying as more and more people are able to use technology to connect to the internet.

HOW

Cyberbullying is an ocurrence that can happen using various forms of technology, may it be the computer, gaming console, or more. Out of all these, the cell phone is the most used form of technology for cyberbullying. Over 80% of teens now use a cell phone, making it one of the most popular forms of communication. This has also opened the door for cell phones to be used for bullying. It has blurred the lines for many parents, who may not be sure how cell phones can be used to cyber bully.

WHY

"Cyberbullying is becoming more of a danger to the youth as the Internet is becoming an intergral part of our lives. It is easier to communicate than ever before, meaning that bullying, unfortunately, is more possible. This topic pushes us to address how do we moderate an online environment to prevent the tradgedies caused by cyberbullying, and how much regulation can we really implement before we begin to violate the freedoms we have as Americans."
